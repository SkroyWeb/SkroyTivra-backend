Our Mission & Vision

We believe everyone deserves access to nutritious food, while reducing the estimated 1.3 billion tons of food wasted globally each year.

Our decentralized community kitchens collect surplus food from restaurants, events, and households, transforming potential waste into nourishing meals for vulnerable community members.

By fostering a collaborative ecosystem of volunteers, donors, and recipients, we're building resilient local food systems that address both hunger and waste simultaneously.

How It Works

Our decentralized community kitchen model connects food donors with volunteers and recipients through a simple yet effective process.

1

Food Source Identification

Surplus or excess food is identified (before it goes to waste). Donors log in to the platform and fill in details: type, quantity, freshness, pickup time. AI tags perishable vs. non-perishable and prioritizes accordingly.

2

Food Listing & Verification

The food is listed on the platform and geo-tagged using donor‚Äôs location. Optionally, images or expiry tags are added for food validation. The system runs a quick check (via AI/manual moderation) for food safety standards.

3

Smart Matching with NGO/Recipient

The system matches food listings with nearby NGOs/recipients based on quantity, need, and distance. Urgent or perishable items get priority matching.

4

Volunteer/Delivery Partner Allocation

Nearby volunteers/delivery partners are notified of a pickup request. They accept, reach the location, verify food pickup, and proceed to deliver.

5

Delivery & Feedback Loop

Food is delivered to the recipient NGO or community kitchen. Delivery completion is confirmed via app check-in and optional photo. Both donor and recipient provide feedback and ratings.

6

Impact Dashboard & Analytics

All stakeholders can view their impact stats: Meals saved CO‚ÇÇ emissions prevented (optional) No. of successful deliveries Leaderboards, badges, and certificates for volunteers. Businesses get sustainability reports to showcase CSR.
